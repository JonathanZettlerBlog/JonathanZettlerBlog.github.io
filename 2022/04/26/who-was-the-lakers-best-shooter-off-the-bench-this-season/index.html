Who Was The Lakers Best Shooter Off The Bench This Season

2022-04-26

The Los Angeles Lakers had a very disappointing 2021-2022 season after acquiring many new names during the off-season. It is expected that the Lakers will look to move many of their players during this off-season, but there are still some players who had good seasons despite the rough season. My objective here is to look at the Lakers best shooters off the bench to see who might be a bright idea to resign for next season.

The first statistic I chose to look at was Field Goal percentage. I built a bar chart of the Lakers bench players field goal percentages. Players on this chart had to have played over 41 games, but started less than 41 games to qualify them as bench players. They also had to have shot more than 250 field goals throughout the season. As you can see from the bar chart, Malik Monk led the Lakers bench in field goal percentage and Talen Horton-Tucker was the worst Lakers bench player in terms of field goal percentage.

The next statistic I looked at was Three-Point Percentage. I constructed a Table showing the Lakers bench players’ Three-Point Percentage. The qualifications for a bench player stayed the same where the players had to have played in more than 41 games, but start in less than 41 games. The bench players on this table had to have shot more than 100 Three-Pointers.

Once again, Malik Monk led the pack for the Lakers bench players. Monk had the highest three-point percentage at 39.1%. Also, Talen Horton-Tucker came in last place once again with a three-point percentage of 26.9%.

One of the best measures of a player’s shooting is true shooting percentage, which takes into account free throws, three-pointers, and field goals. Points per game is another measure that cannot be discounted when looking at shooting, I decided to create a scatterplot showing the Lakers bench players true shooting percentages and their points per game.

As you can see from the scatterplot above, Austin Reaves had the highest true shooting percentage of all the Lakers bench players, but had the 4th most points per game. The most impressive on this chart of the Lakers bench players was Malik Monk who had the second highest true shooting percentage and the highest points per game.

On all three charts that I constructed to figure out who was the best shooter on the Lakers bench, Malik Monk ranked first in the first two charts and pretty much tied for first in the third chart. I think it is very to say based on these charts, that Malik Monk was the Lakers best shooter from the bench this past Laker’s season.
